# Test prompts for Person B (AI validation)

One-sentence **What if?** prompts per incident (`incidents.json` order). Use on `/simulate/[incidentId]` after seed.

## Order 1 — Arrival of Arahat Mahinda (Anuradhapura)
1. Mahinda turns back at Mihintale and Buddhism never takes root in Lanka.
2. Devanampiya Tissa rejects the sermon and keeps the old animist state cult.
3. A rival sage converts the court to Jainism instead of Buddhism.

## Order 2 — Battle of Vijithapura (Anuradhapura)
1. Elara wins the battle and keeps the north divided for another century.
2. Dutugemunu dies in the assault and the campaign to unify the island stalls.
3. A truce splits the island into two kingdoms along the Mahaweli.

## Order 3 — The Chola Invasion (Anuradhapura)
1. Mahinda V repels the Cholas and Anuradhapura remains the capital two more centuries.
2. The Sinhalese court flees to Rohana early and preserves a rival southern kingdom.
3. Chola occupation becomes a permanent province of the Rajendra empire.

## Order 4 — Reign of Parakramabahu the Great (Polonnaruwa)
1. Parakramabahu dies before unification and Polonnaruwa never becomes the capital.
2. The Parakrama Samudra is never built and irrigation collapses in the dry zone.
3. A coup installs a isolationist monk-king who bans foreign trade.

## Order 5 — Invasion of Kalinga Magha (Polonnaruwa)
1. Magha is defeated at the gates and Polonnaruwa survives intact.
2. Refugees establish a stronger kingdom in the wet zone instead of fragmenting.
3. Magha converts to Buddhism and rules as a legitimate Sinhalese king.

## Order 6 — Battle of Danture (Mahanuwara / Kandy)
1. The Portuguese win and capture Kandy within a decade.
2. Vimaladharmasuriya is killed and the highlands fall to Catholic mission states.
3. A Dutch alliance preempts Portugal and colonizes the coast first.

## Order 7 — The Kandyan Convention (golden demo)
1. The chieftains tear up the treaty and rally behind the deposed king against the British.
2. The British offer a protectorate with a puppet king and avoid total annexation.
3. A popular uprising in the hills forces the British to withdraw to the coast only.

## Order 8 — Assassination of Franz Ferdinand (WWI)
1. The assassin hesitates and the motorcade escapes Sarajevo.
2. Austria issues an ultimatum but accepts mediation and avoids war with Serbia.
3. The Black Hand succeeds in killing both Ferdinand and the governor the same day.

## Order 9 — Sinking of the Lusitania (WWI)
1. Germany cancels unrestricted submarine warfare after global outrage.
2. The United States enters the war in 1915 instead of 1917.
3. A secret Anglo-American convoy system prevents further liner sinkings.

## Order 10 — The Zimmermann Telegram (WWI)
1. The telegram is never decrypted and the US stays neutral through 1918.
2. Mexico accepts Germany's offer and raids the southwestern United States.
3. British intelligence exposes the plot early and Germany apologizes publicly.

## Order 11 — Treaty of Versailles (WWI)
1. The Allies offer a mild peace and Germany rejoins European politics in the 1920s.
2. Germany refuses to sign and fighting resumes in 1919 on the Western Front.
3. Wilson's Fourteen Points are adopted fully and colonial mandates are abolished.
